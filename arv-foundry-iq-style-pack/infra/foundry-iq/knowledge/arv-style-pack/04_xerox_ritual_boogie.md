# Xerox Ritual Boogie

## Definition

Xerox Ritual Boogie is the ARV style where an electro-boogie silhouette is treated as a damaged photocopy, a motion fossil, and a scanner artifact. The dance is not shown as full choreography. Instead, one micro gesture becomes a visual machine event.

## Required Anchors

- black electro-boogie silhouette
- damaged photocopy / Xerox paper
- cyan scanner band
- magenta chromatic copies
- misregistered duplicate arms or bodies
- analog film dust and paper grain
- hard contrast
- controlled snap-back to a clean body
- 4 to 6 second seamless loop

## Core Visual Mechanisms

### Scanner Reveal
The body is visible only inside a cyan scanner strip. Outside the strip, the figure returns to darkness.

### Misregistered Copies
One small movement generates 5 to 9 cyan-magenta duplicate silhouettes. They are offset by a few pixels and printed slightly wrong.

### Wet Paper Copies
Duplicates peel sideways like damp photocopy sheets hanging from invisible threads.

### Chronophotography Fan
Ghost silhouettes fan outward from the dancer, each showing a different phase of the same micro movement.

### Controlled Implosion
All ghost bodies pull back to the central silhouette, resolving into one hard freeze.

## Typical Prompt Sentence

A central black electro-boogie silhouette appears printed onto wet photocopy paper inside a vertical amber window; one minimal shoulder twitch releases cyan-magenta misregistered paper copies that hang sideways like damp Xerox strips, CRT bleed gathering at the frame edges before all duplicates collapse back into one hard black end pose.

## Never Do

- no full dance routine
- no breakdance sequence
- no frantic glitch spam
- no random pixel chaos
- no strong text overlay
- no glossy neon club scene
