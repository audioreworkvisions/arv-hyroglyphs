# ARV Motion and Loop Grammar

## Core Loop Shape

darkness -> reveal -> micro movement -> artifact reaction -> controlled collapse -> hard freeze -> seamless return

## Duration

- 4 to 6 seconds.
- One dominant event only.
- First and last frame must be loop-compatible.
- End pose may be identical to first frame or hold a faint afterimage that can resolve cleanly.

## Camera Rules

Preferred:
- locked camera
- symmetrical wide shot
- fixed poster composition
- extremely slow deliberate push only when needed

Avoid:
- fast camera movement
- frantic edits
- shaky handheld
- full choreography
- crowd chaos
- busy multi-event movement

## Micro-Movement Vocabulary

Use one per scene:
- shoulder pop
- elbow lock
- wrist snap
- tiny arm isolation
- chest pop
- slow bodywave
- one head twitch
- one blink
- one shadow slide
- one scanner pass
- one halo compression
- one duplicate collapse

## Payoff Rules

Every loop should have a clear payoff:
- duplicates slam back into one body
- scanner reveals forbidden gesture
- op-art ring appears then dissolves
- shadow returns to alignment
- amber window compresses
- cyan-magenta copies collapse
- silhouette freezes harder than before

## Safety

- no strobe
- no rapid blinking
- no hectic pacing
- no very fast rotation
- no nervous flicker
- no chaotic particle storm
