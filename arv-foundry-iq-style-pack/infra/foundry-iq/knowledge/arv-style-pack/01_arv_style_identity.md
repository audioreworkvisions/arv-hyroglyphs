# ARV / Hyroglyphs Style Identity

## Core Identity

Audioreworkvisions / Hyroglyphs is an audiovisual techno world where visual prompts behave like raw GIF loops, transmissions, measurement systems, damaged archives, rave flyers, absurd underground reality fragments, and living stillframes.

The image should not feel like generic AI fantasy, glossy cyberpunk, corporate motion graphics, luxury sci-fi wallpaper, or stock-video realism. It should feel like a discovered signal from an underground broadcast system that briefly made one unforgettable visual mistake.

## Must Keep

- Hypnotic nocturne loop language.
- Dark or uncluttered space as the default stage.
- One dominant visual mechanism per scene: form, color slip, scanner event, duplicate collapse, pressure pulse, or snapback.
- Strong first-frame readability.
- Controlled, loop-clear motion with visible return to frame one.
- Analog texture: dust, scanlines, paper grain, toner noise, CRT bleed, matte bloom.
- Techno as frequency, ritual, archive, signal, body movement, and abstract shape behavior.
- Images that can work as OBS visuals and as thumbnail-ready frames.

## Preferred Emotional Register

- Mysterious, focused, nocturnal.
- Ritualistic but not generic occult.
- Technological but not sterile.
- Surreal but readable.
- Trippy, raw, and readable.
- Underground, strange, and clean enough for livestream branding.

## Avoid Drift

Do not turn ARV into:
- generic neon cyberpunk city
- fantasy wizard scenes
- random psychedelic chaos
- glossy commercial DJ branding
- stock-video realism
- bland sci-fi spaceship wallpaper
- default AI fantasy art
- busy screens full of unreadable HUD noise
