# ARV / Hyroglyphs Style Identity

## Core Identity

Audioreworkvisions / Hyroglyphs is an audiovisual techno world where visual prompts behave like transmissions, rituals, measurement systems, damaged archives, rave flyers, and living stillframes.

The image should not feel like generic AI fantasy, glossy cyberpunk, corporate motion graphics, or stock-video sci-fi. It should feel like a discovered signal from an underground broadcast system.

## Must Keep

- Hypnotic nocturne loop language.
- Dark or uncluttered space as the default stage.
- One dominant visual mechanism per scene.
- Strong first-frame readability.
- Controlled, loop-clear motion.
- Analog texture: dust, scanlines, paper grain, toner noise, CRT bleed, matte bloom.
- Techno as frequency, ritual, archive, signal, and body movement.
- Images that can work as OBS visuals and as thumbnail-ready frames.

## Preferred Emotional Register

- Mysterious, focused, nocturnal.
- Ritualistic but not generic occult.
- Technological but not sterile.
- Surreal but readable.
- Trippy but calm.
- Underground but clean enough for livestream branding.

## Canonical ARV Concepts

- Signal transmission.
- Frequency as reality.
- Machine memory.
- Damaged archive.
- Visual ritual.
- Stillframe ceremony.
- Body as waveform.
- Gesture as data.
- Motion as photocopy error.
- Rave archaeology.
- Techno mythology.
- PEACE LOVE TECHNO.

## Avoid Drift

Do not turn ARV into:
- generic neon cyberpunk city
- fantasy wizard scenes
- random psychedelic chaos
- glossy commercial DJ branding
- stock-video realism
- bland sci-fi spaceship wallpaper
- default AI fantasy art
- busy screens full of unreadable HUD noise
