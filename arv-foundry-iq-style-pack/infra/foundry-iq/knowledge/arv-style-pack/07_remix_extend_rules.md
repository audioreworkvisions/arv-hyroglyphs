# Remix and Extend Rules

## Remix Mode

When remixing a reference:
Preserve:
- composition logic
- main subject category
- color-temperature relationship
- texture family
- motion density
- one dominant event
- OBS readability

Vary:
- specific setting
- secondary motifs
- palette accents
- scanner direction
- duplicate count
- type of micro movement
- artifact reaction

Do not destroy:
- silhouette clarity
- style family
- loop grammar
- negative space
- ARV identity

## Extend Mode

When extending a story:
- continue the same visual world
- keep motif continuity
- repeat at least one anchor texture or light mechanism
- introduce exactly one new visual idea
- preserve the previous scene's emotional register

## Style Taste Handling

Use style as a family resemblance, not as a rigid template.
Choose 1 to 3 anchor traits per scene.
Do not stack every ARV motif at once.

## When Reference Style Is Cartoon Graffiti

Keep:
- thick black outlines
- sprayed wall texture
- sunset / teal / magenta color logic
- playful creature silhouettes

But ARV-fuse it with:
- darker lighting
- signal overlays
- cyan/red rim light
- lower density
- cinematic framing

## When Reference Style Is Dark Cyber Monk

Keep:
- hooded figure
- ritual hands or still posture
- red/cyan halo
- grain and scanlines
- heavy shadow

But avoid:
- generic villain pose
- too much armor detail
- fantasy wizard tropes
