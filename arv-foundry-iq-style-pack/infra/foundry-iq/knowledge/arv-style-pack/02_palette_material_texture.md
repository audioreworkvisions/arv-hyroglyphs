# ARV Palette, Material, Texture Rules

## Primary Palette Families

### Nocturne Signal
- velvet black
- midnight indigo
- deep cobalt
- cyan halo
- magenta edge bleed
- low red signal glow
- chalk white

### Amber Xerox Ritual
- black paper void
- sunburnt amber
- rust orange
- wet toner black
- cyan scanner band
- magenta chromatic copy
- parchment beige
- dirty white bloom

### Graffiti Rave Cartoon Fusion
- sunset orange
- deep pink sky
- yellow desert light
- turquoise / teal water or night
- black outline
- dirty wall texture
- sprayed magenta and lime accents

### Dark Sci-Fi Transmission
- black / charcoal
- cyan rim light
- emergency red
- dim teal
- smoky grey
- dirty white typography

## Material Cues

Use these as concrete visual anchors:
- damaged photocopy on black paper
- wet Xerox surface
- toner dust
- paper fibers
- old rave flyer grain
- sprayed wall texture
- concrete wall scratches
- fogged glass
- CRT scanlines
- VHS softness
- chromatic misregistration
- matte halation
- low-frame rotoscope drift
- analog rain streaks

## Light Rules

- Light should reveal, scan, slice, or ritualize the subject.
- Prefer one key light mechanism: scanner band, amber window, cyan portal, red/cyan halo, projector bloom.
- Glow should be controlled, not glossy.
- Avoid over-bright bloom that destroys silhouette readability.

## Texture Density

Balanced density:
- subject: crisp
- edges: unstable
- background: textured but not chaotic
- overlays: minimal and meaningful
